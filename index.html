It is distance compounded with timing: two fencers can stand within killing range and be perfectly safe, because the moment is wrong for either blade. Pluto and Neptune have held exactly this stance for four billion years. Their orbits cross — Pluto spends twenty years of every orbit inside Neptune's — and they are never, ever close.
